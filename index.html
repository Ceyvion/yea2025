Story arcs

How Afropop is moving listeners in 2025

Joy, Independence, and Resilience shape Afropop Worldwide’s editorial calendar. These pillars guide fundraising stories, podcasts, and archive projects drawn from achievements data and fresh field reporting.

Joy

Joy: The Living Soundtrack

Afropop Worldwide’s 37th year is a celebration of unbridled joy—dance floors and living rooms where communities find connection and release. From tsapiky riffs to Afro-Lusophone club nights, every story invites listeners to move, laugh, and learn.

  • Madagascar Medley spotlighted Damily, Jaojoby, and rare field recordings from an overlooked island. Listen now
  • Afro-Lisbon and the Lusophone Atlantic followed second-generation artists in Lisbon as they create world-class dance music and question identity. Read the feature
  • Planet Afropop: The Other Sound of Kinshasa uncovered musique de recherche innovators building DIY instruments. Explore the episode

“Afropop’s mission is to increase the profile of music from Africa and its diaspora and ensure the benefits flow back to the artists and their home countries.” — uDiscover Music

Put more joy in the world
Independence

Independence: Editorial Freedom, Listener-Powered

When the National Endowment for the Arts abruptly ended a 38-year grant, Afropop doubled down on its nonprofit model. The Vision Fund and community support keep culture coverage ad-free and unfiltered.

  • NEA funding cuts became a rallying cry as listeners filled the gap to keep the show on air. See the announcement
  • The Vision Fund aims to raise $1.5 million for new producers, social hires, educational resources, and archive digitization. Read the plan
  • External endorsements from Feedspot and uDiscover prove independent, mission-driven media can top global rankings. Check our rankings

“Afropop explores music around the world with a 4.8/5 rating and tens of thousands of followers on social platforms.” — Feedspot

Keep culture ad-free
Resilience

Resilience: Sustaining Memory & Amplifying Under-Reported Scenes

Afropop’s longevity is built on resilience—preserving musical heritage and telling stories that might otherwise vanish. New formats and archival work ensure these sounds outlast the news cycle.

  • Field Recordings of Hugh Tracey chronicled the preservation of 250 LPs of traditional African music and inspired new sessions. Revisit the episode
  • Off the Beaten Track featured Malawi’s Grammy-nominated Zomba Prison Project and Burkina Faso’s vintage “Bobo Yéyé” bands. Hear the stories
  • Living Room Sessions and a proposed ADA-compliant digital museum preview new ways to open the archive. Request the proposal

“Afropop ventures into seldom-heard corners… featuring the Zomba Prison Project and vintage sounds from Burkina Faso.” — Afropop

About Afropop Worldwide

Story-driven radio celebrating Africa and the diaspora

For more than three decades we have amplified artists, preserved oral histories, and connected audiences to the soundscapes shaping global culture.

Human-first storytelling

Hosts and producers travel with musicians and community guides, capturing intimate performances and conversations in their hometowns.

Deep-dive journalism

Our Hip Deep series unpacks the social forces behind the music with historians, activists, and culture bearers.

Training new voices

Fellowships mentor emerging African journalists in documentary production, mixing, and narrative craft.
